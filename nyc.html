New York City

Local knowledge from a NYC resident. The real spots, the best eats, and what's actually worth your time.

Category 01

Museums

Art
  • MoMA ★ top pick
    One of the world's most prestigious art museums. First stop for art lovers — an absolute world-class collection.
  • The MET
    Better if you want a mix of art and historical artifacts. While there, check out Central Park's Conservatory Garden and walk by the exterior of the Guggenheim.
Non-Art
  • Museum of Natural History
    World-class, great for all ages. Highlights:
    • Top floor full of dinosaur fossils
    • Hall of Gems and Stones (personal favorite)
    • Ocean room with a giant whale replica
    • Planetarium — great 5-min show
Category 02

Walking Routes

Route A · West Side
  • Start at Hudson Yards and The Vessel
  • Walk down the High Line — old railroad track converted into an elevated park
  • Chelsea Market — great food hall. Thai restaurant is solid; the line for Los Tacos No. 1 is worth it (get the Al Pastor)
  • Little Island — a few minutes from Chelsea Market, unique floating park on the Hudson
Route B · Midtown
  • Times Square → Rockefeller Center, passing Radio City Music Hall on the way
  • Best view of Rockefeller is from Fifth Avenue — also where you go for high-end shopping
  • Grand Central Station is a short walk — beautiful building worth seeing
Route C · Brooklyn
  • Walk the Brooklyn Bridge
  • Explore DUMBO — don't miss the iconic spot under the bridge (just Google it)
  • Main Street Park — carousel and some of the best city skyline views
  • Randolph Beer — great view of the city, excellent beer selection, games
Neighborhood · Lower Manhattan
  • SoHo / Prince St. — culture, influencers, celeb sightings. Where the cool people hang out.
  • Washington Square Park — personal favorite, great glimpse into the city's energy and diversity
  • Uncommons — cool board game café nearby
  • Percy's Pizza — my favorite NYC-style pizza in the city
Category 03

Restaurants

Thai
  • Som Tum Der ★ best Thai — Lower East Side. One of the best Thai restaurants in the city. Don't miss this one.
  • Aiyada — inside Chelsea Market. Great if you're doing Route A.
  • Wondee Siam — Hell's Kitchen. Solid and reliable.
Japanese
  • Sugarfish ★ must-try
    God-tier omakase for under $50 — an absolute steal. No reservations, walk in and put your name in.
  • Nami Nori — hand rolls. One of my personal favorites. A unique and excellent experience.
New York Classics
  • Katz's Delicatessen — Lower East Side. Get the Pastrami sandwich. Non-negotiable.
Korean
  • Turntables — Korean fried chicken in Koreatown. Great vibe, excellent food.
Category 04

Broadway

  • Moulin Rouge ★ most spectacular — Flashy, visual, pop and classic music. A step above the usual Broadway experience.
  • Chucked — Funny and family-friendly. A safe, entertaining pick.
  • TodayTix — Use this app for tickets. Solid pricing vs. buying direct. Hamilton is incredible but very expensive, just FYI.
Category 05

Bars & Nightlife

  • Gallow Green — Beautiful rooftop bar with a lush green/jungle aesthetic. Great cocktails and atmosphere.
  • 230 Fifth Rooftop Bar — For clubbing with a view. One of the best views of the Empire State Building in the city.
  • Cellar Dog — Dive bar with live jazz, ping pong, foosball, chess, and shuffleboard. One of those places you stay way longer than planned.
  • Somewhere Nowhere — Another fun clubbing option. Good energy.
