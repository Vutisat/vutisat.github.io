Category 01

Museums

Art
Top Pick · Art

MoMA

One of the world's most prestigious art museums. Highly recommended as the first stop for art lovers — an absolute world-class collection.

World-Class
Art + History

The MET

If you want a mix of art and historical artifacts rather than pure contemporary art, the MET is your pick. While you're there, check out Central Park's Conservatory Garden and walk by the exterior of the Guggenheim building nearby.

Non-Art
Family-Friendly · Science

Museum of Natural History

Another world-class museum, great for all ages. Highlights include:

  • ▸Top floor full of dinosaur fossils
  • ▸Hall of Gems and Stones (personal favorite)
  • ▸Ocean room with a giant whale replica
  • ▸Planetarium with a great 5-min show
Could spend a full day here
Category 02

Walking Routes

Route A · West Side

Hudson Yards → Chelsea

  • Start at Hudson Yards and The Vessel
  • Walk down the High Line — a beautiful old railroad track converted into an elevated park
  • Stop at Chelsea Market — great food hall. The Thai restaurant is solid; the long line for Los Tacos No. 1 is worth it (get the Al Pastor)
  • Visit Little Island — just a few minutes from Chelsea Market, a unique floating park on the Hudson
Route B · Midtown

Times Square → Grand Central

  • From Times Square, walk to Rockefeller Center — pass Radio City Music Hall on the way
  • Best view of Rockefeller is from Fifth Avenue
  • Fifth Avenue is also where you go for high-end shopping
  • Grand Central Station is a short walk — beautiful building worth seeing, there's a bathroom in the basement
Route C · Brooklyn

Chinatown → DUMBO

  • Walk the Brooklyn Bridge
  • Explore DUMBO — don't miss the iconic Instagram spot under the bridge (just Google it)
  • Visit Main Street Park and go by the carousel for some of the best city skyline views
  • There's a food hall nearby in DUMBO
  • For drinks: Randolph Beer — great view of the city, excellent beer selection, and a bunch of games
Neighborhood · Lower Manhattan

SoHo / Washington Square Park

  • SoHo / Prince St. is where all the cool people hang out — culture, influencers, celeb sightings
  • Washington Square Park is a personal favorite — great glimpse into the city's energy and diversity
  • Nearby is Uncommons, a cool board game café
  • Percy's Pizza is nearby — my favorite NYC-style pizza in the city

Japanese
Sushi · Multiple Locations

Sugarfish

Highly, highly recommended. You can get god-tier omakase for under $50 — an absolute steal. No reservations, walk in and put your name in.

Must Try
Japanese · Hand Rolls

Nami Nori

One of my personal favorites in the city. They specialize in amazing hand rolls. A unique and excellent experience.

New York Classics

Katz's Delicatessen

The OG New York experience. Get the Pastrami sandwich. This is non-negotiable.

NYC Institution
Korean

Category 05

Bars & Nightlife

Dive Bar · Live Music

Cellar Dog

A great dive bar with live jazz. Also has ping pong, foosball, chess, and shuffleboard. Really good vibes — one of those places you stay way longer than planned.

Cocktail Bar · Rooftop

Gallow Green

A beautiful rooftop bar with a lush green/forest/jungle aesthetic. Great cocktails, great atmosphere — a step up from the usual bar.

Garden Rooftop Vibes

For clubbing with a stunning view — one of the best views of the Empire State Building in the city. A go-to for nights out with a wow factor.
