Thailand

Personal recommendations from someone who grew up there. No tourist traps, just the good stuff.

Region 01

The North

Mountains and forests. Chiang Mai and Chiang Rai are the main bases. If you can find accommodation near Phu Chi Fa — do it.

  • Phu Chi Fa ★ must-do
    You climb high enough to be above the clouds. At sunrise, you're looking out over a sea of clouds — one of the most surreal sights in Thailand. You must arrive the night before to catch the morning show.
  • Wat Rong Khun ★ must-do
    The White Temple. All-white architecture with mirror inlays — extraordinarily beautiful. Do not skip this.
  • Golden Triangle
    Where Thailand, Laos, and Burma converge. You can stand at the border of three countries at once. Good accommodation nearby — don't let the crowds deter you.
  • Doi Tung
    Beautiful gardens built to honor the King's mother. Worth visiting for the scenery.
  • Doi Inthanon
    Highest peak in Thailand. The grand pagoda built in honor of the King and Queen is impressive. Worth it if you have time, but not top priority.
Region 02

The South

The most beautiful beaches are along the Andaman Coast — Trang, Krabi, and surrounding islands. Know where to go and what to skip.

Skip Phuket. Tourist trap with a massive party scene. Nothing is authentically Thai, beaches are subpar, prices inflated. Unless you're specifically looking for clubs and raves, there are much better options.
  • Koh Lanta ★ best base
    Popular but genuinely beautiful. Great range of accommodation. From here you can do day tours:
    • Koh Rok — one of the most beautiful beaches, barely developed, you can even camp there
    • Snorkeling & diving — swimming with schools of thousands of fish
    • Sea cave tours
    • Koh Mook — an emerald green beach inside a cave. Absolutely amazing.
    • Seasonal waterfall tours (best during rainy season)
  • Koh Phi Phi
    Another solid choice. More upscale and developed than Koh Lanta — genuinely beautiful, just pricier.
  • Ao Phang Nga National Park
    Home to Koh Tapu (James Bond Island). The limestone karsts rising from emerald water are iconic.
Many smaller islands are beautiful but remote — more adventurous than relaxing. Plenty have campgrounds if that's your style.
Region 03

Central — Bangkok

  • Wat Phra Kaew & Grand Palace ★ must-do
    Go to all the famous temples, but prioritize this one. The Grand Palace complex is stunning — especially lit up at night.
  • Siam District
    Heart of modern Bangkok. Siam Paragon and Central World are both larger than Mall of America and a block from each other. Also: Siam Discovery and Siam Center for a younger/trendier scene.
  • Amphawa Floating Market
    About an hour south of Bangkok. The most recommended floating market — liveliest with the most to see. Gets extremely crowded; go early.
Important

Cultural Rules

  • Don't touch anyone's head or hair — the head is considered the most sacred part of the body.
  • Don't step on money or books — feet are considered the lowest, most disrespectful part of the body.
  • Don't criticize the monarch — this is literally illegal under Thai law. Not a joke.
  • Don't touch any Buddhist sculpture — even seemingly casual contact is deeply disrespectful.
  • Keep your wallet in your front pocket — never the back pocket, even if you think you're careful.
  • Pause during the national anthem — it plays every day at 6pm and 8am. Remove your hat. People stop what they're doing.
