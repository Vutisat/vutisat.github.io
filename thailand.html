Thailand

Personal recommendations from someone who grew up there. No tourist traps, just the good stuff.

Back to Portfolio
Region 01

The North

Northern Thailand is a world of mountains and forests. Chiang Mai and Chiang Rai are the two major cities and easy bases for accommodation. If you can find a legitimate place near Phu Chi Fa — highly recommended.

01 / HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

Phu Chi Fa

You climb so high up the mountains that you're above the clouds. At sunrise, you're looking out over a sea of clouds — one of the most surreal sights in all of Thailand. You must arrive the night before to catch the morning show.

Must stay overnight
02

Doi Tung

A famous and beautiful tourist destination featuring a stunning garden built to honor the King's mother. Well worth the visit for the scenery alone.

Garden + Nature
03

Doi Inthanon

Doi means mountain peak. This is the highest point in Thailand, and will one day serve as the burial site of the King and Queen. There's a grand "jaydee" (pagoda) structure built in their honor. Worth it if you have time, but not the top priority.

04 / HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

Wat Rong Khun

One of the most famous Buddhist temples in Thailand, beloved by both locals and tourists. The all-white architecture with mirror inlays is extraordinarily beautiful — do not skip this one.

Highly Recommended
05

Golden Triangle

The point where Thailand, Laos, and Burma converge — you can stand at the border of three countries at once. A neat place to cross into Laos or Burma if you want. Plenty of tourists means plenty of accommodation, and it's actually quite nice — don't let the crowds deter you.

3-Country Border
Region 02

The South

The most beautiful beaches are in Trang, Krabi, or really anywhere along the Andaman Coast. There are countless beaches and islands (called Koh or Ko). Know where to go and what to skip.

Skip Phuket

Phuket is a tourist trap with a massive party scene. Nothing is authentically Thai, the beaches are subpar, and prices are inflated. Unless you're specifically looking for clubs and raves, there are much better options.

ISLAND / HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

Koh Lanta

Very popular with tourists, but genuinely beautiful. Great range of accommodation from luxury hotels to budget rooms. From here you can take day tours to:

  • ▸Koh Rok — one of the most beautiful beaches available, barely developed, you can even camp there
  • ▸Snorkeling & diving — swimming with schools of thousands of fish
  • ▸Sea cave tours
  • ▸Koh Mook — an emerald green beach inside a cave. Absolutely amazing.
  • ▸Seasonal waterfall tours (best during rainy season)
Best Island Base Camp
ISLAND

Koh Phi Phi

Another popular and genuinely good choice. More upscale and well-developed for tourism. Still very beautiful — just more polished and pricier than Koh Lanta.

More Upscale
NATIONAL PARK

Ao Phang Nga National Park

Home to Koh Tapu — also known as James Bond Island, famously featured in The Man with the Golden Gun. The dramatic limestone karsts rising out of emerald water are iconic.

Off the Beaten Path

Many smaller islands are beautiful but remote and not very convenient — more adventurous than relaxing. Plenty of islands have campgrounds if that's your style.

Region 03

Central — Bangkok

SHOPPING / FOOD

Siam District

The heart of modern Bangkok. Explore Siam Paragon and walk down to Central World — both are larger than Mall of America, and they're a block from each other. Also nearby: Siam Discovery and Siam Center (more of a youth/trendy scene).

TEMPLES / HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

Wat Phra Kaew & Grand Palace

Go to all the famous temples, and make Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha) a priority. The Grand Palace complex is stunning — and even more breathtaking lit up at night.

Don't Miss
DAY TRIP

Amphawa Floating Market

About an hour south of Bangkok. The most recommended floating market — the liveliest with the most to see. Fair warning: it gets extremely crowded. Go early.

~1hr from Bangkok
Important

Cultural Rules

Don't

Touch anyone's head or hair — the head is considered the most sacred part of the body.

Don't

Step on money or books — feet are considered the lowest and most disrespectful part of the body.

Don't

Criticize the monarch — this is literally illegal under Thai law. Not a joke.

Don't

Touch any Buddhist sculpture — even seemingly casual contact is considered deeply disrespectful.

Don't

Keep your wallet in your back pocket. Nobody does it. Front pocket only — even if you think you're careful.

Do

Pause during the national anthem — it plays every day at 6pm and 8am. Remove your hat. People stop what they're doing.
